WHAT IS A LIKERT SCORE?
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  A Likert scale rates each answer from 1 to 5 on five criteria:

  1 = Very Poor  |  2 = Poor  |  3 = Acceptable
  4 = Good       |  5 = Excellent

  CRITERION 1 -- Factual Accuracy (weight=30%)
    Do the numbers and facts in the prediction match
    the ground truth? Numbers within 3% count as a match.

  CRITERION 2 -- Completeness (weight=25%)
    Does the prediction cover all key points from the
    ground truth? Measures content word coverage.

  CRITERION 3 -- Clarity (weight=20%)
    Is the answer clearly written? Checks sentence
    length (ideal: 15-25 words) and word variety.

  CRITERION 4 -- Relevance (weight=15%)
    Does the answer stay on topic for the question?
    Measures question keyword coverage in the answer.

  CRITERION 5 -- Scientific Depth (weight=10%)
    Does the answer use appropriate biomedical terms?
    Checks for terms like IDR, pLDDT, Pfam, proline, etc.

  Overall Likert = weighted mean of all five criteria
