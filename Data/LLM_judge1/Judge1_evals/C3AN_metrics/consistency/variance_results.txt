WHAT IS OUTPUT VARIANCE?
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Output variance measures how much LLM1 predictions DEVIATE
  from the ground truth across six dimensions.
  Low variance = model stays close to ground truth.
  High variance = model drifts significantly from ground truth.

  METRIC 1 -- LENGTH VARIANCE (weight=20%)
    Difference in word count between prediction and GT.
    A prediction much longer or shorter than GT loses points.

  METRIC 2 -- VOCABULARY VARIANCE (weight=15%)
    Difference in unique word counts.
    High vocabulary variance means different terminology used.

  METRIC 3 -- NUMERIC DRIFT (weight=30%)
    How far off predicted numbers are from GT numbers.
    e.g. GT says 29.1% but prediction uses very different numbers.
    Most heavily weighted -- numeric accuracy is critical in science.

  METRIC 4 -- LEXICAL DIVERSITY (weight=15%)
    Difference in type-token ratio (unique words / total words).
    Measures whether pred and GT have similar writing complexity.

  METRIC 5 -- SENTENCE COUNT VARIANCE (weight=10%)
    Difference in number of sentences.
    Measures structural similarity of the two answers.

  METRIC 6 -- INFORMATION DENSITY (weight=10%)
    Difference in content word ratio.
    High density = information-packed. Low = more filler words.

  Overall variance score: 0.0 (no variance) to 1.0 (maximum variance)
    < 0.10 : VERY LOW VARIANCE
    < 0.25 : LOW VARIANCE
    < 0.50 : MODERATE VARIANCE
    < 0.75 : HIGH VARIANCE
    >= 0.75 : VERY HIGH VARIANCE
