WHAT IS COSINE SIMILARITY?
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Cosine similarity measures the angle between two text vectors.
  Score of 1.0 = identical meaning.
  Score of 0.0 = no shared concepts.

  How it works:
  1. VECTORIZE  -- Each text is converted to a TF-IDF vector.
     TF-IDF weights words by importance to the specific text
     vs the whole corpus. Domain terms like 'disorder', 'pLDDT',
     'IDR' get higher weight than common words.

  2. NORMALIZE  -- Each vector is scaled to unit length.

  3. DOT PRODUCT -- cos(angle) = v1 dot v2 / (|v1| x |v2|)
     High dot product = vectors point in same direction
     = texts talk about the same concepts.

  Score interpretation:
    0.9 - 1.0 : VERY HIGH -- nearly identical semantic content
    0.7 - 0.9 : HIGH      -- strong semantic overlap
    0.5 - 0.7 : MODERATE  -- partial overlap
    0.3 - 0.5 : LOW       -- weak overlap
    0.0 - 0.3 : VERY LOW  -- little to no overlap
