WHAT IS BERTSCORE?
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  BERTScore measures semantic similarity between two texts using
  contextual token embeddings from a BERT model.

  Why BERTScore over NAUR or cosine TF-IDF?
    NAUR and TF-IDF only match EXACT words. BERTScore captures
    MEANING even when different words express the same concept.
    Example:
      'protein backbone flexibility'
      'conformational freedom of the chain'
    These score near 0 on NAUR but high on BERTScore.

  Why BiomedBERT specifically?
    Generic BERT is trained on Wikipedia and books.
    BiomedBERT is trained on PubMed abstracts, so it understands
    biomedical terms like IDR, pLDDT, Pfam, intrinsically
    disordered proteins accurately.
    (Gu et al. 2021, ACM CHIL -- Domain-specific LM pretraining)

  How it works:
    1. EMBED   -- BiomedBERT encodes each token contextually
    2. MATCH   -- Each pred token matched to most similar GT token
    3. PRECISION = mean(max similarity of pred->GT matches)
    4. RECALL    = mean(max similarity of GT->pred matches)
    5. F1        = harmonic mean of precision and recall

  Reference: Zhang et al. (2020) BERTScore: Evaluating Text
  Generation with BERT. ICLR 2020.

  Score interpretation:
    0.90 - 1.0 : VERY HIGH -- semantically near-identical
    0.80 - 0.90 : HIGH      -- strong semantic match
    0.70 - 0.80 : MODERATE  -- good semantic overlap
    0.60 - 0.70 : LOW       -- partial semantic match
    < 0.60      : VERY LOW  -- weak semantic similarity
