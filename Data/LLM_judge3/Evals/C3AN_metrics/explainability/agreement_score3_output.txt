INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Judge A (Retrieval Fidelity) is expected to score HIGH for
  extractive fallback mode because answers ARE the retrieved
  passages concatenated directly.

  Judge B (Question Relevance) is expected to show more variance
  because BiomedBERT retrieval sometimes pulls passages that are
  topically related but do not directly answer the specific question.

  Judge C (Scientific Validity) should be moderate-to-high since
  the KB passages are grounded in DisProt experimental data, but
  contradictory co-retrieved passages can reduce validity scores.

  Low Kappa between Judge A and Judge B reflects the core tension
  in RAG systems: high retrieval fidelity does not guarantee high
  question relevance when the retriever pulls semantically similar
  but question-mismatched passages.
