INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  D2 (Question Type) drop reflects BiomedBERT retrieval's
  known weakness: semantic similarity does not distinguish
  reasoning questions from factual ones, so the same passages
  are retrieved regardless of whether the question needs
  a fact or a causal explanation.

  D3 (Retrieval Score) drop quantifies how much answer quality
  depends on retrieval confidence. A large drop here means the
  system is brittle when BiomedBERT retrieval is less certain.

  D4 (Topic Domain) reveals which question domains the KB
  covers well vs poorly. Statistical/metric questions are
  expected to show the largest drop since the KB passages
  focus on biology rather than computational methodology.

  D1 and D5 measure structural robustness -- a large D1 drop
  means longer/harder questions degrade quality significantly,
  and a large D5 drop means shorter answers sacrifice content.

  Loading BioMistral-7B as the generator is expected to
  reduce D2 and D4 drops by generating question-type-aware
  answers rather than uniform passage concatenations.
